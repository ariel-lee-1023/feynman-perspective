# Feynman's named constructs & recurring frames

**Guess → compute → compare** (the method). Find a new law by guessing it, computing the consequences of
the guess, and comparing those consequences to experiment/observation. "If it disagrees with experiment it
is wrong" — regardless of the guess's beauty or the guesser's name. (Clusters c13, c04, c16.)

**Cargo-cult science.** Work that reproduces the *forms* of science — apparatus, papers, jargon — while
missing the essential thing: the utter honesty and care not to fool yourself. Like South Sea islanders
building runways and waiting for planes that never land. (Cluster c04.)

**The two integrities / "leaning over backwards."** (1) Ordinary honesty: don't lie. (2) The extra
scientific integrity: actively report everything that might make you wrong — rival explanations, doubts,
things you checked — not just what supports you. The second is the hard one. (Clusters c04, c10, c06.)

**"You are the easiest person to fool."** The first principle: not fooling yourself comes before not
fooling others, and is much harder. (Clusters c04, c06.)

**Name vs. thing.** Knowing what something is called — in any number of languages — is not knowing
anything about the thing itself. Learned from his father via the bird. (Clusters c05, c07, c17, c01.)

**Clear, not precise.** The goal of language is a clearly communicated idea, not maximal precision. Add
precision only where a genuine doubt exists, and put it exactly there. (Cluster c17.)

**Any way that works.** No single correct method for a problem; invent your own, use whatever tool gets
the right answer. Freedom in method, none in the answer. (Clusters c17, c01, c16.)

**"A different box of tools."** Advantage often comes from having non-standard methods others lack (e.g.
differentiating under the integral sign), so you crack what their standard tools couldn't. (Cluster c01.)

**"Reality must take precedence over public relations, for nature cannot be fooled."** The Challenger
minority-report maxim: institutional confidence cannot override physical fact. (Clusters c06, c11.)

**The pleasure of finding things out.** The reward is the process of discovery itself, not its uses,
prizes, or recognition. (Title theme; clusters c07, c03, c09, c16.)

**Imagination in a terrible strait-jacket.** The constraint on guessing: a new idea must agree with
everything already known, disagree somewhere new, and be right in that disagreement. Beauty and
simplicity *guide* the guess (when it's right, "more comes out than goes in"); experiment alone
*judges* it. Beauty proposes, experiment disposes. (Cluster c13.)

**Prejudice vs. absolute certainty.** Bias is permissible and harmless — if it's wrong, accumulating
experiments will keep annoying you until it can't be disregarded. Absolute certainty is fatal, because
it lets you disregard the accumulation permanently. Have opinions; keep them falsifiable. (Cluster c13.)

**Asymmetric scrutiny (the Millikan diagnostic).** You don't detect bias by finding an error; you detect
it by asking whether you looked equally hard in both directions. Post-Millikan electron-charge
measurements crept toward the truth because discrepant results were audited and agreeable ones weren't.
(Cluster c04.)

**Shrinking effects (the ESP diagnostic).** A real effect survives better instruments; an artifact
evaporates under them. When someone argues that demanding repeatability is unreasonable, the game is
over. (Cluster c04.)

**Replication before variation.** Own the baseline before you own the comparison: reproduce the original
result in your own setup before changing the variable, or you can't tell your change from your setup.
(Cluster c04.)

**"You're being used."** If you'd publish an advisory finding only when it flatters the party who asked,
you aren't giving advice — the asymmetry launders your name into their conclusion. (Cluster c04.)

**Precision that manufactures doubt.** Added precision can *lower* clarity ("color the picture of the
ball red" creates an ambiguity "color the ball red" never had). Test whether it does. (Cluster c17.)
